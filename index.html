Week 0 of 10 0%

Goal: Reach basic Korean proficiency in 10 weeks. You'll learn to read and write Hangul, build foundational grammar and vocabulary, hold simple conversations, and understand Korean sentence structure.

Primary Textbook: How to Study Korean (free, online). Unit 0 (Hangul) + Unit 1 (Lessons 1-25), supplemented with audio from Talk To Me In Korean and video lectures from Coursera.

Weekly rhythm: ~5 hours/week. 2-3 HTSK lessons, 1 TTMIK audio lesson, practice exercises, and a weekly check-in quiz below.

10-Week Benchmarks

  • Read and write all Hangul
  • Form basic sentences (S-O-V)
  • Use particles (은/는, 이/가, 을/를)
  • Conjugate verbs (present/past/future)
  • Count with both number systems
  • Hold a simple self-introduction
  • Understand ~300 vocabulary words
  • Read a simple Korean paragraph

Week 2 Check-in Quiz

1. What is the basic Korean sentence order?

Correct! Korean uses Subject-Object-Verb order. "I rice eat" not "I eat rice."

2. The particle 은/는 marks the _____ of a sentence.

Correct! 은/는 is the topic-marking particle.

3. Which verb means "to be" in Korean?

Correct! 이다 means "to be."

Week 3 Check-in Quiz

1. Which particle marks the object of a sentence?

Correct! 을/를 marks the direct object.

2. Korean verb/adjective dictionary forms always end in ___.

Correct! All Korean verbs and adjectives end in 다 in dictionary form (먹다, 크다, 가다, etc.).

3. In Korean, 크다 (to be big) functions as what part of speech?

Correct! 크다 is a descriptive verb (adjective). Korean adjectives conjugate like verbs.

Week 4 Check-in Quiz

1. To conjugate past tense, you add _____ to the verb stem.

Correct! 았/었 creates past tense (먹다 → 먹었다).

2. "I study at the library." Which particle goes with library? 도서관___

Correct! 에서 marks the location where an action takes place. 에 marks a static location or destination.

3. "I ate." = 저는 밥을 _____.

Correct! 먹었어요 is the polite past tense of 먹다 (to eat).

Midpoint Review Quiz

1. 덥다 (to be hot) is an example of which irregular?

Correct! 덥다 is ㅂ-irregular: 덥 + 어요 → 더워요.

2. 빠르다 (to be fast). The adverb form is:

Correct! 빨리 (quickly) is the common adverb form.

3. In 나는 책을 읽었어요, what does 을 mark?

Correct! 을 marks 책 (book) as the direct object. "I read a book."

Week 6 Check-in Quiz

1. Phone numbers use which number system?

Correct! Phone numbers, dates, and money use Sino-Korean numbers.

2. 셋 becomes _____ when used before a counter.

Correct! 셋 → 세 before counters (세 개, 세 명).

3. Telling time uses which number systems?

Correct! Hours use Native Korean (한 시, 두 시), minutes use Sino-Korean (삼십 분).

Week 7 Check-in Quiz

1. "I want to eat" = 먹_____.

Correct! 먹고 싶어요 = "I want to eat." Stem + 고 싶다.

2. The short negation form places _____ before the verb.

Correct! 안 먹어요 = "I don't eat." 못 means "can't" (different meaning).

Week 8 Check-in Quiz

1. "I can speak Korean" = 한국어를 말할 _____.

Correct! -ㄹ 수 있다 expresses ability/possibility.

2. To turn a verb into a noun, you add -는 _____.

Correct! 먹는 것 = "eating" (the act of eating). -는 것 nominalizes verbs.

Week 9 Check-in Quiz

1. "More than" in Korean comparisons uses the particle _____.

Correct! 한국어가 일본어보다 더 어려워요 = "Korean is harder than Japanese."

2. "I have a book" = 책이 _____.

Correct! 있다 = "to exist/to have." 없다 = "to not exist/to not have."

Comprehensive Final Quiz

1. "If I do" = _____ (하다 + ~면)

Correct! 하 + 면 = 하면 (if I do).

2. "I am a student" in polite speech:

Correct! 저 (humble "I") + 는 (topic) + 학생 (student) + 이에요 (polite "to be").

3. How many speech levels does Korean have?

Correct! Korean has 6 speech levels, though modern usage mainly uses 2-3 (해요체, 합쇼체, 해체).
